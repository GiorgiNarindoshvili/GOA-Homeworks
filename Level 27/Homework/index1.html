The Most Expansive cars In The World

German Cars

Mercedes Maybach-When thinking about luxury cars, a brand that will never fail to spring to mind is Maybach. Originally an independent luxury company separate from Mercedes, Maybach became a sub-brand of Mercedes in 2015, allowing the company to continue to produce the best luxury cars on the market. One of the newer models created when Maybach was still independent in 2003 is the iconic Maybach 57, a car which, along with the Maybach 52 set the standard for luxury automobiles around the world in the early 2000s. The Mercedes S-Class range was already a great car, but the increased size, opulence, and performance took it to a whole new level.

2019 Bmw 7 Series-BMW is a manufacturer that has been at the forefront of the German automotive scene, producing countless sedans, wagons, and SUVs that appeal to such a wide market. In 2022, BMW released the latest generation of the largest sedan in their fleet, the 7 Series G70. This model was designed with luxury and comfort at the forefront, with the car maximizing every element that leads to that high-end experience only German cars can offer. The interior follows a flowing, monolithic design style to achieve a prestigious feel when inside the car, topping anything else the company had produced beforehand.

2023 BMW 8 Series Gran Coupe-The newest mass-production car to feature on our list, the '23 BMW 8 Series Gran Coupe, without a doubt manages to rival the 7 series regarding outright luxury, being a testament to what modern technology can do for the ultimate driving experience. Providing elegance in an understated yet effective manner, the interior of the '23 8 series Gran Coupe draws from the rest of the manufacturer's luxury fleet without sacrificing any of the performance that the model has been known for over the years. The performance is provided by the silky-smooth powertrain options and responsive steering, making the car wonderful to drive. Very few cars have managed to pair speed with luxury and comfort like the 8 Series.
